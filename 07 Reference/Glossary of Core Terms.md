## Attribute
A numerical representation of a Seeker’s physical or mental capabilities. The four Attributes are Fitness, Dexterity, Wisdom, and Intelligence.

## Test
A roll made to determine the outcome of an action using 1d20 + Attribute. You succeed if the result meets or exceeds the DC.

## Save
A roll made to resist hazards, relic effects, or environmental threats using 1d20 + Attribute.

## Difficulty Class (DC)
A target number representing how hard a task, hazard, or activation is. Meeting or exceeding the DC is a success.

## Advantage
Roll 2d20 and take the higher result.

## Disadvantage
Roll 2d20 and take the lower result.

## Passive Score
A value equal to 10 + Attribute, used when a roll is not made.

## Action
One of the two decisions a Seeker may take on their turn, such as attacking, switching rows, activating a relic, or using Aid.

## Reaction
A special response a character may take once per round when triggered.

## Formation
The gridless combat structure consisting of Frontline and Backline positions.

## Frontline
The row that receives melee attacks and protects the Backline.

## Backline
The protected row for ranged, support, or fragile Seekers.

## Broken Formation
A state in which enemies may bypass normal targeting rules because the Frontline has collapsed.

## HP (Health Points)
A measure of a character’s vitality. At 0 HP, a Seeker becomes Unconscious.

## AC (Armor Class)
The number an attack must meet or exceed to hit a Seeker.

## Resistance
A flat reduction to incoming damage.

## Condition
A temporary state that affects combat performance or survivability.

## Relic
A mysterious object that can be activated using Attribute tests.

## Synchronization
A Seeker’s resonance with a relic category, allowing attunement and granting bonuses.

## Relic Slot
A limit on how many relics a Seeker may attune at the same time.

## Strain
A complication or weakening effect caused by relic overuse or unstable activation.

## Tier
A measure of a relic’s power, difficulty, and potential strain.
