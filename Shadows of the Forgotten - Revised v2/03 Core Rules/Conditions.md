
Conditions represent temporary states of impairment, hindrance, or danger.

---

## Entangled
- Cannot switch rows  
- –2 to Dexterity saves  

---

## Stunned
- Lose **1 Action** on your next turn  

---

## Bleeding
- Take **1d4 damage** at the end of each turn until healed  

---

## Poisoned
- –1 on all rolls  

---

## Blinded
- Attacks have **Disadvantage**  

---

## Disoriented
- –1 to Intelligence tests  
- –2 to Initiative if persistent  

---

## Wounded
- Recover only **half HP** from rests  

---

## Unconscious

An Unconscious character:
- cannot act  
- auto-fails Fitness and Dexterity saves  
- loses Frontline/Backline placement  
- is auto-hit by melee attacks unless the GM rules otherwise  

---

## Dying

A Dying character:
- remains unconscious  
- cannot be stabilized by mundane means  
- can only recover through healing effects  

---

## 0 HP Rule

At 0 HP, a character becomes **Unconscious**, not Dying.

Further harm immediately causes the **Dying** condition.

---

## Reviving

### Aid (2 Actions)
Restore **1 HP** (no roll).  
The ally awakens and may act next turn.

### Healing Effects
Any effect restoring HP removes **Dying** immediately.
